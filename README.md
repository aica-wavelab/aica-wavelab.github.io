## What is AICA? 

The Digitization College "Artificial Intelligence in Culture and Arts" (AICA) aims to equip students at the University of Music and Performing Arts Munich and the University of Applied Sciences Munich with competencies that enable them to actively shape AI-based processes in various artistic and creative fields and to drive AI innovations in the cultural and creative industries.

## What is the project workshop ?

In the winter semester 2023 / 2024, the AICA project workshop will take place at Wavelab, in which HMTM and HM students will work in interdisciplinary teams to develop their own projects and ideas for the use of AI technologies in art, culture and the creative industries. The course aims to equip students with skills that enable them to actively shape AI-based processes in various artistic and creative fields of play, as well as to drive AI innovations in the cultural and creative industries. 
Teams will receive input from various experts on AI applications within music, theater/performance, and the creative industries at the beginning of the course. The students will then devote themselves to the creative processes of idea development and implementation under the guidance and input of agile coach Holger Neumann and with the help of precisely so-called agile methods (e.g., design thinking). The external experts Nina Maria Stemberger from Artes Mobiles, Katja Littow from hl-studios, as well as experts from the AICA team (Dr. Esther Fee Feichtner, Dr. Benedikt Zönnchen and Helena Held) will be on site and accompany the projects.
In addition, the course will visit the XR-HUB Bavaria together to give the students insights into the latest technological applications.
The teams will jointly give a presentation on their developed projects at the end and write a 10-page group term paper describing the development of the project. In addition, there is the possibility to extend projects in the context of a bachelor/master thesis and - who knows - maybe there will be a spin-off or two.



The project workshop is intended to serve the conception and implementation of own projects at the interface of AI in art and culture. In the project workshop, students work in interdisciplinary teams accompanied by technology and culture experts and supported by agile coaches on concrete AI solutions for challenges and ideas from the cultural and creative industries.
